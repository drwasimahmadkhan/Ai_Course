Computer Vision & Ethics

Image Understanding and Responsible AI

Image Classification and Recognition

Image classification assigns labels to images. Deep CNNs learn hierarchical visual features.

CNN Architectures

AlexNet (breakthrough 2012) → VGG (depth) → ResNet (skip connections) → EfficientNet (scalable)

Key Techniques

  • Data Augmentation: Rotation, crop, color jitter. Prevents overfitting.
  • Transfer Learning: Pre-train on ImageNet; fine-tune on target task.
  • Ensemble: Combine multiple models for robustness.
Accuracy
Modern CNNs exceed human performance on ImageNet (~98%).

Object Detection and Localization

Object detection finds and classifies objects. Predict bounding boxes + class probabilities.

Approaches

R-CNN Family
Region proposals + classification. Slower; accurate. Faster R-CNN, Mask R-CNN variants.
YOLO / SSD
Single-shot: predict boxes + classes in one pass. Fast; good for real-time.

Real-World Use

  • Autonomous Driving: Detect pedestrians, vehicles, signs
  • Surveillance: Person, object detection in video streams
  • Medical Imaging: Locate lesions, tumors in scans

Semantic and Instance Segmentation

Segmentation assigns class labels to pixels. Semantic: per-class. Instance: per-object.

Architectures

  • Fully Convolutional Networks (FCN): End-to-end pixel classification
  • U-Net: Encoder-decoder with skip connections. Medical imaging breakthrough.
  • Mask R-CNN: Extend Faster R-CNN with mask prediction. SOTA instance segmentation.

Applications

  • Medical: Organ, lesion segmentation from CT/MRI
  • Autonomous: Drivable area, lane detection
  • Scene Understanding: Furniture, room types

Fairness and Bias in AI Systems

Fairness ensures AI systems treat groups equitably. Bias systematic favoring of certain groups.

Sources of Bias

Training data biases, feature engineering, model optimization, deployment contexts

Bias Types

Representation Bias
Underrepresented groups in training data. Models perform worse.
Measurement Bias
Proxy variables correlate with protected attributes.
Aggregation Bias
One-size-fits-all model ignores subgroup differences.

Mitigation Strategies

  • Data Collection: Ensure diverse, representative datasets
  • Fairness Constraints: Optimize subject to equity constraints
  • Auditing: Test across demographic groups; report disparities

Transparency, Explainability and Accountability

Explainability helps humans understand AI decisions. Accountability assigns responsibility.

Explainability Methods

LIME

Local linear approximations. Explain individual predictions.

SHAP

Shapley values; game-theoretic fairness. Feature importance.

Attention

Visualize weights; see what model focuses on.

Challenges

  • Trade-off: Complex models better at accuracy; harder to explain
  • Responsibility: Who is liable for AI failures? System, deployer, user?
  • Regulation: GDPR, AI Act require explainability; enforcement unclear

Privacy, Security and Robustness

Privacy protects individual data. Security guards against attacks. Robustness resists adversarial examples.

Privacy Techniques

Differential Privacy
Add noise to gradients; guarantee mathematical privacy. Accurate + private.
Federated Learning
Train on distributed devices; share only model updates. Data stays local.

Adversarial Robustness

  • Attacks: Imperceptible perturbations fool classifiers
  • Defense: Adversarial training; certified defenses
  • Challenge: Trade-off between robustness and clean accuracy

AI Ethics Framework and Governance

AI ethics principles guide responsible development. Governance implements oversight.

Ethical Principles

  • Beneficence: Maximize positive impact
  • Non-maleficence: Minimize harm
  • Autonomy: Respect human agency; don't undermine
  • Justice: Fair treatment; benefit sharing
  • Explainability: Transparent decision-making

Implementation

Governance Mechanisms

Ethics boards for review, impact assessments before deployment, continuous monitoring, clear redress processes for harms

Summary: Computer Vision & Ethics
